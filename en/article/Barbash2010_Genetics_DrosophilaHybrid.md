# Ninety Years of Drosophila Hybrid Sterility: From Sturtevant to the Hmr/Lhr Genes

## Citation (NLM)
Barbash DA. Ninety years of Drosophila melanogaster hybrids. Genetics. 2010;186(1):1-8. doi:10.1534/genetics.110.121459

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.110.121459](https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.110.121459)

---

## Background

Hybrid incompatibility is the most direct entry point for understanding the genetic basis of speciation. The study of hybrid incompatibility between *Drosophila melanogaster* and *D. simulans*, begun by A. H. Sturtevant in the 1920s, has over ninety years been resolved down to the molecular level.

The Dobzhansky-Muller incompatibility (DMI) model explains that when two populations evolve independently, alleles that function without problem within each population can interact deleteriously (epistasis) when brought together in a hybrid, causing sterility or inviability. This Perspectives article reviews ninety years of research, centered on the discovery and characterization of the key genes that support this model — *Hmr* (Hybrid male rescue) and *Lhr* (Lethal hybrid rescue).

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Classical genetics: re-examination of Sturtevant's original hybrid-incompatibility crosses
2. Gene mapping: localization and cloning of the *Hmr* and *Lhr* genes
3. Functional analysis: biochemical characterization and localization of the Hmr and Lhr proteins
4. Interspecific transgenesis: introducing *D. melanogaster* chromosomal fragments into the *D. simulans* genome to assay the incompatibility phenotype
5. Regulatory divergence analysis: comparison of gene-expression patterns between the two species

---

## Results

- **Sturtevant's legacy**: the lethality and sterility of *D. melanogaster* × *D. simulans* hybrids, first described in 1920, became over subsequent decades the key system for understanding Dobzhansky-Muller incompatibility.
- **The Hmr and Lhr genes**: *Hmr* (Hybrid male rescue), located in *D. melanogaster*, and *Lhr* (Lethal hybrid rescue), located in *D. simulans*, were identified as major causes of hybrid lethality. Hybrids carrying rescue alleles at both genes have the lethal phenotype suppressed (rescued).
- **Demonstration of Dobzhansky-Muller incompatibility**: Hmr and Lhr function normally within their own species, but interact deleteriously when both are present together in a hybrid genome.
- **Gene transposition**: some incompatibility genes appear to have undergone functional change via inter-chromosomal transposition during speciation.
- **Regulatory divergence**: not only protein-sequence changes but also divergence in gene-expression regulation contributes to hybrid incompatibility.

---

## Perspective

This Perspectives article systematically reviews ninety years of speciation-genetics research and shows how the transition from classical to modern molecular genetics came to empirically support the Dobzhansky-Muller incompatibility model. The discovery of Hmr and Lhr is significant as the first identification of concrete gene pairs that drive speciation. Future work must determine how these incompatibility genes acquired new functions within each species, and whether this accelerates speciation or is merely a byproduct.

### Future Research Directions

1. Elucidate the biochemical mechanism of the Hmr-Lhr protein interaction
2. Identify additional hybrid-incompatibility gene pairs and build out the Dobzhansky-Muller incompatibility network
3. Compare the relative contributions of regulatory divergence and protein-sequence divergence to speciation
4. Analyze the population-genetic properties (selection coefficients, fixation rates) of hybrid-incompatibility genes in natural populations
