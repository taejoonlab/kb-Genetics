# Genomic and Genetic Insights into Mendel's Pea Genes

## Citation (NLM)
Feng C, Chen B, Hofer J, et al. Genomic and genetic insights into Mendel's pea genes. Nature. 2025;642:980–989. doi:10.1038/s41586-025-08891-6

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-08891-6](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-08891-6)

---

## Summary

This landmark paper from a Chinese–British–French consortium (Agricultural Genomics Institute at Shenzhen / John Innes Centre / INRAE) presents a comprehensive population genomics and trait-mapping study that completes the molecular characterization of all seven traits Mendel studied in 1865, 160 years after his discovery.

**Study design:** A core diversity panel of 697 Pisum accessions was assembled from the John Innes Pisum Germplasm Collection, including 500 core diversity representatives, 130 additional breeding lines, and 67 wild taxa (P. elatius, P. fulvum, P. abyssinicum). Whole-genome short-read resequencing generated ~80 Gb clean reads per accession (~20× coverage), yielding a genomic variation map encompassing 154.8 million high-quality SNPs against the ZW6 reference assembly. Population structure analysis identified 8 major Pisum groups (G1–G8), with a reticulated network structure reflecting complex crossing and introgression history.

**Confirming and extending the four characterized genes:**
- **R (seed shape):** Single strong GWAS signal at PsSBE1. Full-length sequence of the Ips-r element (1,021 bp) resolved. The R locus is also the primary determinant of seed protein content variation.
- **I (cotyledon color):** Two key alleles characterized: i-1 (5,696-nt TAR Ty1-Copia LTR retrotransposon insertion) and i-2 (novel 408-bp promoter deletion) in the Mg-dechelatase (SGR) gene.
- **A (flower color):** Several novel alleles identified, including a rare intragenic suppressor that restores the most abundant a allele to wild-type — implying the a allele has existed long enough for this second-site mutation to occur at extremely low probability.
- **Le (stem length):** Five haplotypes identified; le variants found exclusively in haplotype 1, carrying the known G→A substitution at chr. 5:639,901,919.

**Resolving the three uncharacterized traits:**

**(1) Pod color (Gp):** GWAS and linkage mapping showed all yellow-podded lines carry an ~100-kb genomic deletion adjacent to the chlorophyll synthase gene ChlG (also known as Psat03G0413700). The deletion removes one Ogre LTR retrotransposon, a partial NBS-LRR gene, and exons 5, 6, and 7 of a TIR-NBS-LRR gene, with the ChlG gene itself structurally intact in all gp lines. However, in gp pods, intergenic splicing between the truncated TIR-NBS-LRR and ChlG generates transcript fusions and intron read-throughs, reducing functional ChlG transcripts to ~6% of wild-type levels. A TILLING mutant carrying a premature stop codon in ChlG (W121*) was obtained; a complementation cross between gp/gp and this heterozygous TILLING mutant produced 10 yellow-podded F1 plants out of 16, all of which carried the ChlGW121* allele. This establishes that Gp corresponds to ChlG.

**(2) Pod shape (P and V):**
- **P:** GWAS identified an 8.3-Mb peak on chromosome 1, refined to 0.92 Mb by F2 linkage mapping. The most significant gene was Psat01G0420500 (PsCLE41), encoding a dodeca-CLE peptide identical to Arabidopsis TDIF (tracheary element differentiation inhibitory factor). An in-frame premature stop codon (R79*) upstream of the TDIF motif fully explains the p phenotype. This CLE41 peptide is predicted to interact with PXY receptor kinase to specify sclerenchyma formation via the TDIF–PXY–WOX signaling pathway.
- **V:** The GWAS interval (chr. 6, 629–631 Mb) overlapped with a 1.25-Mb F2 mapping interval. All parchmentless (v) accessions clustered in a single haplotype for PsMYB26, the pea orthologue of Arabidopsis MYB26, a master transcription factor for secondary wall thickening and lignification. An ~23-kb Ogre-type LTR retrotransposon insertion upstream of PsMYB26 is present in all P/P v/v genotypes, and PsMYB26 expression is markedly reduced in mutant lines. Further confirmation is needed.

**(3) Flower position / fasciation (Fa):** GWAS identified a broad signal on chromosome 4 (0–40 Mb), refined to 1.33 Mb by BSA and F2 mapping. Within this interval, only Psat04G0031700 (PsCIK2/3) co-segregated with fasciation. The fa allele carries a 5-bp exonic deletion causing a frameshift and premature stop codon. PsCIK2/3 encodes a CLAVATA3 INSENSITIVE RECEPTOR KINASE co-receptor, known for maintaining shoot apical meristem structure. A modifier locus (Mfa) on chromosome 6 was also found: fa/fa mfa/mfa double homozygotes appear wild-type, explaining some previously puzzling 2-locus segregation ratios reported for fasciation.

**Additional agronomic traits:** A broad GWAS of 72 agriculturally relevant traits identified hundreds of significant marker–trait associations, including 14 previously cloned genes and 22 loci mapped to within an average 12-Mb interval. Notable new loci include PsOs1 (organ size; controls pod width and seed weight) and the D locus for axil ring anthocyanin pigmentation (a MYB gene cluster).

## Significance in Introduction Context

Why this paper matters at the introduction to genetics:
- Completes 160 years of genetics by providing the molecular identities of all seven traits Mendel used to discover his laws, closing the most famous open question in classical genetics
- The Gp locus demonstrates that "a gene" may be a functional genomic region rather than a simple coding sequence — Gp corresponds to a deletion that disrupts transcription of a downstream gene via transcript fusion, raising conceptual questions about how genes are defined
- Essential course reading because it shows with modern tools that Mendel's experimental choices were excellent: all seven traits are major-effect loci, not minor-effect variants, and dominance reflects functional vs. non-functional alleles in every case

## Future Research Directions

Unresolved questions and future research directions from this paper:

- Establishing the precise molecular mechanism by which the Ogre retrotransposon insertion upstream of PsMYB26 reduces its expression in v alleles — additional functional validation beyond VIGS is needed
- Identifying the Mfa modifier gene and understanding how it suppresses the fasciation phenotype, potentially through epistatic interactions with PsCIK2/3 in the CLV3-WUS pathway
- Applying long-read sequencing and single-cell omics to fully characterize how large structural variations and transposable elements mediate phenotypic change, including in gp and other structurally complex loci
- Translating pea trait genetics to improvement of other legume crops (soybean, lentil, chickpea) using the haplotype–phenotype associations established here
