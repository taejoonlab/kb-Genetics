# A Reference Genome for Pea Provides Insight into Legume Genome Evolution

## Citation (NLM)
Kreplak J, Madoui MA, Cápal P, et al. A reference genome for pea provides insight into legume genome evolution. Nat Genet. 2019;51(9):1411–1422. doi:10.1038/s41588-019-0480-1

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-019-0480-1](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-019-0480-1)

---

## Summary

Garden pea (Pisum sativum, 2n = 14) is the organism Gregor Mendel used to discover the laws of genetics. Despite its unique historical significance, a reference genome had been lacking due to its large size (~4.45 Gb) and extraordinarily high repeat content (~76%). This paper reports the first annotated chromosome-level reference genome assembly for pea (cultivar 'Caméor', v.1a).

**Assembly strategy**: The team combined Illumina short reads (281× coverage), PacBio long reads (13×), BAC library profiling, flow-cytometry-isolated single chromosomes, BioNano optical maps, and high-density genetic maps to anchor scaffolds into seven pseudomolecules representing the seven pea chromosomes. The assembly spans 3.92 Gb (~88% of the estimated genome size); the missing ~12% is attributable largely to collapsed tandem satellites and highly repetitive mobile elements.

**Genome composition**: Repetitive elements constitute ~83% of the genome, dominated by Ty3-gypsy Ogre LTR retrotransposons (Class I). A total of 44,756 protein-coding genes were predicted, with 92.3% BUSCO completeness from Embryophyta single-copy orthologs. Notably, pea has the highest proportion of singleton genes (54%) among the legumes surveyed, which may explain why Mendel found so many distinct morphological mutants for his crossing experiments.

**Paleohistory of legume genomes**: Comparative synteny analysis across 21 eudicot species reconstructed the Ancestral Legume Karyotype (ALK) with 19 proto-chromosomes. The pea genome differentiated from this ancestor through at least three chromosomal fissions, four fusions, and one translocation between chromosomes Ps1 and Ps5.

**Pisum genome structure evolution**: Flow-sorted single chromosomes from three wild accessions (P. fulvum '703', P. elatius '721', P. humile '711') were resequenced and mapped to the 'Caméor' pseudomolecules. A major translocation involving chromosome 5 was identified in all wild accessions: P. elatius/humile shows a translocation between chromosomes 1 and 5, while P. fulvum shows a translocation between chromosomes 3 and 5. The ancestral Pisum karyotype likely resembled P. elatius/humile.

**Population genomics and seed storage proteins**: Resequencing 42 Pisum accessions identified 17.2 million high-quality SNPs. Nucleotide diversity decreased 3.4-fold from wild accessions to cultivars (π = 8.2 × 10⁻⁴ vs. 2.4 × 10⁻⁴), with elevated FST and LD, indicating moderate domestication bottleneck. Seed storage protein (legumin, vicilin, convicilin) gene clusters were mapped and found to co-localize with previously reported SSP quantity QTLs, establishing a genomic basis for seed protein content variation.

## Significance in Introduction Context

This paper's significance at the introduction of the field:
- [Provides the first chromosome-level genome for Mendel's original model organism, opening the molecular investigation of all seven Mendelian traits and bridging classical genetics with modern genomics]
- [Explains why the pea genome is so large — massive LTR retrotransposon amplification, particularly of Ogre elements — and why the Fabeae tribe uniquely harbors such large genomes among legumes]
- [Essential reading for a genetics course because it directly connects Mendel's pea experiments of the 1860s to 21st-century genomics, demonstrating how a reference genome transforms research possibilities]

## Future Research Directions

Unresolved questions and future research ideas raised by this paper:

- [The missing ~12% of the genome: can ultra-long PacBio/Nanopore reads fully resolve centromeric satellite arrays and telomeres to produce a telomere-to-telomere assembly?]
- [What causal relationship exists between the Fabeae tribe divergence (~24.7–17.5 Ma) and the burst of Ty3-gypsy Ogre retrotransposon amplification that drove genome size expansion?]
- [Three of Mendel's seven traits (pod color GP/gp, pod form V/v, flower position Fa/fa) remain uncloned at the molecular level — can this reference genome enable their positional cloning?]
