# The Am-tra2 Gene Is an Essential Regulator of Female Splice Regulation at Two Levels of the Sex Determination Hierarchy of the Honeybee

## Citation (NLM)
Nissen I, Müller M, Beye M. The Am-tra2 gene is an essential regulator of female splice regulation at two levels of the sex determination hierarchy of the honeybee. Genetics. 2012;192(3):1015–1026. doi:10.1534/genetics.112.143925

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.112.143925](https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.112.143925)

---

## Summary

The honeybee sex determination pathway (csd → fem → Am-dsx) had been established by three prior studies, but a critical mechanistic gap remained: both CSD and Fem proteins lack an RNA recognition motif (RRM), yet they direct the alternative splicing of downstream pre-mRNAs. This implies the existence of an RNA-binding co-factor. Nissen et al. (2012) identify the honeybee ortholog of Drosophila transformer-2, **Am-tra2**, as an essential component of the sex determination hierarchy, required for female splicing of both fem and Am-dsx transcripts. Additionally, they discover an unexpected, sex-determination-independent essential role for Am-tra2 in honeybee embryogenesis.

### Background
In Drosophila, Tra protein alone cannot direct female splicing of dsx pre-mRNA; it requires the RNA-binding protein Tra2 (which contains an RRM flanked by two RS domains) to bind the dsx exonic splicing enhancers and activate a weak female splice acceptor. A tra2 ortholog in the honeybee genome (Am-tra2) had been predicted bioinformatically but never confirmed to be expressed or functional. Given the structural similarity (lack of RRM) between honeybee Csd/Fem and Drosophila Tra, Am-Tra2 was the prime candidate for the missing co-factor.

### Key Experiments and Results

**1. Characterization of Am-tra2 Gene Structure and Transcripts**
- RACE experiments and RT-PCR using cDNA from male and female embryos and pupae yielded six splice variants.
- Three major transcript variants differ in the length of exon 2 (and thus the first RS domain): Am-tra2285 (5 exons, 285 aa), Am-tra2253 (6 exons, 253 aa), Am-tra2234 (6 exons, 234 aa); each has a minor variant differing by a single serine due to an alternative splice acceptor in exon 4.
- All six isoforms share the same domain architecture: RBD flanked by RS1 and RS2 domains.
- Am-tra2285 and Am-tra2284 are most abundantly expressed.

**2. Am-tra2 Transcripts Are Not Sex-Specifically Spliced**
- All six splice variants detected in both male and female embryos and pupae—no sex-specific pattern.
- Expression level peaks in embryonic stages (including before csd is active at 0–6 hours) and substantially decreases at the pupal stage.
- No sex-specific splicing in gonadal tissues (ovary, testis)—contrasting with Drosophila, where male germline-specific Tra-2 variants regulate spermatogenesis.
- Conclusion: Am-Tra2 is a constitutively expressed, ubiquitous splicing regulator available before and throughout the sex determination process.

**3. Evolutionary Divergence of the Am-Tra2 RBD**
- Pairwise amino acid identity of the RBD: 82–85% within Hymenoptera, 61–68% between honeybee and dipteran species.
- Within the RBD, two RNP consensus elements are identified. Three amino acid differences in RNP-1 relative to Drosophila are found.
- The first amino acid of RNP-1—arginine (R) in Drosophila, where mutation abolishes female dsx splicing—is substituted to **lysine (K)** in the honeybee and Nasonia lineage.
- Interpretation: The RNA target-binding specificity of Am-Tra2 has co-evolved with its pre-mRNA targets (fem, Am-dsx) away from the Drosophila dsx repeat elements, which are absent from Am-dsx pre-mRNA.

**4. Am-tra2 Knockdown Reveals an Essential Embryonic Function**
- Injection of 20–200 pg dsRNA-1 or 33–224 pg dsRNA-2 per embryo caused complete failure to reach L1 larval stage in both females (Table 1) and males (Table 2).
- These dsRNA amounts are 4–225 times lower than amounts used in prior csd and fem knockdown studies (900 pg), where no embryonic lethality was observed.
- At 4 pg dsRNA-1 or 56 pg dsRNA-2, a small fraction of larvae hatched (up to ~5%), enabling splicing analysis.
- The lethality is not sex-specific, suggesting it is unrelated to sex determination.
- Conclusion: Am-tra2 has an **essential, sex-determination-independent function** in honeybee embryogenesis—a role not reported for tra2 in any other insect.

**5. Am-tra2 Is Required for Female Splicing of Am-dsx and fem**
- **At 4 pg dsRNA-2** (lowest functional dose): female embryos produce male-type Am-dsx mRNA (absent in untreated controls, which produce only female Am-dsx) → Am-Tra2 is required for female Am-dsx splicing. Female fem mRNA levels are not substantially affected at this concentration.
- **At 33 pg dsRNA-2**: female splicing of fem transcripts is also impaired (reduced productive female fem and reduced nonproductive male fem mRNAs).
- **In haploid males**: injection of 33–67 pg dsRNA-2 eliminates male fem mRNA → Am-Tra2 is also required for male-type splicing of fem.
- Key control: csd and ef-1a gene splicing are unaffected at these doses, ruling out non-specific disruption of splicing machinery.

### Integrated Model
Am-Tra2 is a constitutively expressed RNA-binding co-factor that operates at two levels of the sex determination cascade:
- **Female Am-dsx splicing**: Am-Tra2 (with RBD) + Fem protein → female Am-dsx mRNA → female Dsx transcription factor.
- **Female/male fem splicing**: Am-Tra2 (possibly with CSD or Fem) promotes female fem splicing in females; Am-Tra2 also promotes male fem splicing in males (absence of active CSD/Fem).
- The dual role in both male and female fem splicing is proposed to enhance and stabilize the binary decision, analogous to how a toggle switch benefits from robust opposing forces.

## Significance in Introduction Context

Why this paper completes the molecular mechanism of the honeybee sex determination cascade:
- **Resolves the RRM paradox**: CSD and Fem lack RNA-binding domains; Am-Tra2 provides the missing RRM-containing co-factor that physically interacts with pre-mRNAs and enables the splicing switch.
- **Dual function stabilizes the binary switch**: Am-Tra2 is needed for both the productive female mode and the non-productive male mode of fem splicing. This dual role reinforces the all-or-nothing character of sex determination.
- **Ancestral Tra2 role in dsx regulation**: The conserved requirement for Tra2 proteins in female dsx splicing across insects (Drosophila, dipterans, now honeybees) despite evolution of different binding sites, supports this as an ancestral holometabolous insect function.
- **Unexpected embryonic essential function**: Highlights that genes in sex determination pathways can have additional, broader developmental roles that may constrain their evolutionary diversification.

## Future Research Directions

Open questions raised by this paper:

- What are the precise RNA binding sites of Am-Tra2 on fem and Am-dsx pre-mRNAs? CLIP-seq or systematic evolution of ligands by exponential enrichment (SELEX) experiments are needed to identify the evolved cis-elements.
- Does Am-Tra2 physically interact with heteroallelic CSD proteins and/or Fem protein? Co-immunoprecipitation or yeast two-hybrid studies could test the protein-protein interaction model.
- What are the non-sex-determination targets of Am-tra2 in embryogenesis? Transcriptome-wide analysis of Am-tra2 knockdowns at sub-lethal doses could identify essential splicing targets.
- Can the R→K substitution in RNP-1 be functionally swapped between Drosophila and honeybee systems to test whether binding specificity has co-evolved with the pre-mRNA targets?
- Does Am-tra2 have a role in spermatogenesis or oogenesis (beyond what was detected here), perhaps via a subset of the six isoforms that remains to be functionally differentiated?
