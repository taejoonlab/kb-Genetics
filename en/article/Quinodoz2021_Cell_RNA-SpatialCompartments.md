# RNA promotes the formation of spatial compartments in the nucleus

## Citation (NLM)
Quinodoz SA, Jachowicz JW, Bhat P, Ollikainen N, Banerjee AK, Goronzy IN, Blanco MR, Chovanec P, Chow A, Markaki Y, Thai J, Plath K, Guttman M. RNA promotes the formation of spatial compartments in the nucleus. Cell. 2021;184(23):5775-5790. doi:10.1016/j.cell.2021.10.014

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2021.10.014](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2021.10.014)

## Summary

While prior genome-wide studies of nuclear organization focused predominantly on DNA-DNA contacts, this paper by Quinodoz et al. introduces RNA & DNA SPRITE (RD-SPRITE), a dramatically improved version of the original SPRITE method that enables simultaneous, high-resolution mapping of RNA-RNA, RNA-DNA, and DNA-DNA contacts within 3D nuclear structures. The method works through iterative split-and-pool barcoding: crosslinked cells are lysed, contents fragmented, DNA and RNA molecules within each spatial complex tagged with strand-specific adaptors, barcoded through multiple rounds of split-and-pool, then sequenced — with all molecules sharing a barcode assigned to the same SPRITE cluster. Unlike proximity-ligation methods, RD-SPRITE can detect multi-way contacts among molecules not directly adjacent to each other.

Applying RD-SPRITE to mouse embryonic stem cells, the authors identify hundreds of non-coding RNAs (ncRNAs) that form high-concentration territories throughout the nucleus, associated with three major classes of nuclear function: RNA processing, heterochromatin assembly, and gene regulation. For RNA processing, distinct processing hubs are identified: a nucleolar hub containing 45S pre-rRNA, RNase MRP, and dozens of snoRNAs; a spliceosomal hub containing nascent pre-mRNAs, snRNAs, and Malat1; a Cajal body-like hub where scaRNAs co-localize with snRNA gene clusters; and a histone locus body (HLB) hub where U7 snRNA concentrates near histone gene clusters on chromosome 13. In each case, nascent RNA transcription anchors diffusible ncRNAs into these compartments: treatment with actinomycin D (ActD), which inhibits RNA Pol I and Pol II, disrupts the focal localization of snoRNAs, scaRNAs, and U7, and collapses the long-range DNA-DNA contacts between loci organized within each hub.

For heterochromatin assembly, satellite-derived ncRNAs transcribed from major and minor satellite DNA at centromeres and pericentromeres form a hub that recruits HP1 protein. Knockdown of major or minor satellite RNAs by antisense oligonucleotides depletes HP1 from chromocenters, demonstrating a causal role for these ncRNAs in heterochromatin formation. For gene regulation, the authors find that 93% of lncRNAs are enriched within 3D proximity of their transcriptional loci, forming stable nuclear territories (resistant to transcriptional inhibition by flavopiridol). The lncRNA Kcnq1ot1 localizes within the TAD containing its imprinted target genes (Kcnq1, Cdkn1c, Slc22a18, Phlda2) and recruits SHARP/Spen and its HDAC3 complex to silence these targets; deletion of the SHARP-binding site on Kcnq1ot1 de-represses these genes.

The authors propose a unified "seed-bind-recruit" mechanism: ncRNAs form high-concentration territories near their transcriptional loci (seed), bind diffusible regulatory ncRNAs and proteins through high-affinity interactions immediately upon transcription (bind), and thereby concentrate these molecules within specific nuclear compartments (recruit). This mechanism exploits RNA's unique positional advantage — unlike proteins, which are translated in the cytoplasm, ncRNAs accumulate at high concentration near their genomic loci and can thereby spatially organize other molecules.

## Significance in Introduction Context

- Establishes RNA as a widespread and mechanistically active organizer of 3D nuclear architecture, complementing the established roles of proteins (cohesin, CTCF) and DNA topology in genome organization
- Demonstrates that nuclear compartments (nucleolus, HLB, Cajal bodies, chromocenters) are not merely protein-scaffolded structures but depend on nascent RNA transcription to assemble and maintain their organization
- Provides a mechanistic explanation (seed-bind-recruit) for how diffusible regulatory molecules become spatially concentrated within specific nuclear territories despite random Brownian motion
- Links lncRNA-mediated compartment formation to gene regulation and human disease, showing that Kcnq1ot1 organizes an imprinted gene cluster in 3D space to silence target genes — relevant to Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome
- Expands the catalog of functional ncRNAs from a handful of well-studied examples (Xist, Malat1) to hundreds of lncRNAs with defined nuclear territories, suggesting RNA-mediated compartmentalization is a general principle

## Future Research Directions

- Applying RD-SPRITE or similar technologies to diverse cell types and developmental stages to understand how RNA-mediated compartments change during differentiation and how this contributes to cell-type-specific gene expression
- Determining whether the seed-bind-recruit mechanism operates at transcriptional condensates and superenhancers, where enhancer RNA (eRNA) production might similarly spatially organize transcription factors
- Systematically identifying which lncRNA territories depend on nascent RNA (like the processing hubs) versus stable mature RNA (like Kcnq1ot1), and whether these categories correspond to distinct functional mechanisms
- Developing methods to disrupt specific RNA-mediated nuclear territories without affecting overall transcription, to establish causal relationships between RNA territories and gene regulation
- Investigating whether RNA territories are disrupted in cancer or other diseases where lncRNA expression is altered, and whether restoring correct ncRNA localization is therapeutically feasible
