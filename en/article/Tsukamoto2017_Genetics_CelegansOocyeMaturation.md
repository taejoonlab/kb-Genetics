# LIN-41 and OMA Ribonucleoprotein Complexes Mediate a Translational Repression-to-Activation Switch Controlling Oocyte Meiotic Maturation and the Oocyte-to-Embryo Transition in Caenorhabditis elegans

## Summary

Oocyte meiotic maturation in _Caenorhabditis elegans_ is controlled by a precisely timed translational repression-to-activation switch. Tsukamoto et al. (2017) demonstrated that ribonucleoprotein (RNP) complexes containing the RNA-binding proteins LIN-41 and OMA-1/OMA-2 are central mediators of this switch, governing the transition from translational repression in immature oocytes to translational activation during the oocyte-to-embryo transition (OET).

LIN-41 is a TRIM-NHL (Tripartite Motif-NHL domain) family RNA-binding protein that is highly expressed in immature oocytes, where it maintains numerous mRNAs in a translationally repressed state. A key repression target is CDK-1 (cyclin-dependent kinase 1): LIN-41 binds directly to cdk-1 mRNA and inhibits its translation, thereby preventing premature meiotic entry. In contrast, OMA-1 and OMA-2 are activated when oocyte maturation is triggered by a sperm-derived signal (major sperm protein, MSP), and they act to release cdk-1 mRNA from LIN-41-mediated repression.

The study's central experimental findings are as follows. First, LIN-41 and OMA proteins physically interact within the same RNP complex; co-immunoprecipitation (co-IP) and mass spectrometry identified the components of these LIN-41-OMA complexes. Second, RNA immunoprecipitation (RIP) and PAR-CLIP experiments mapped the transcriptome-wide binding targets of LIN-41, revealing that its direct mRNA targets include many transcripts involved in meiotic maturation, cell cycle control, and embryonic development.

Third, analysis of lin-41 loss-of-function mutants showed that absence of LIN-41 causes precocious maturation before the appropriate developmental stage, confirming that LIN-41 functions as a temporal repressor of maturation. Fourth, RNA-seq profiling of lin-41 and oma-1/2 mutants quantified transcriptome-wide changes and identified overlapping as well as distinct target sets for the two regulators.

After meiotic maturation is complete, OMA proteins are rapidly degraded. This allows CPB-1 (a CPEB ortholog) to promote polyadenylation of mRNAs that had been translationally suppressed by LIN-41, converting them to actively translated messages. The transition from LIN-41-dependent repression to LIN-41 degradation and CPB-1-dependent translational activation thus constitutes the core molecular logic controlling the OET.

PAT-seq (poly(A)-tail sequencing) was employed to track polyadenylation states of mRNAs under LIN-41-dependent repression and to monitor changes following maturation, demonstrating that dynamic 3' UTR regulation plays a pivotal role throughout the OET.

This study makes a significant contribution to understanding the mechanisms of TRIM-NHL protein-mediated translational repression, the molecular timing of oocyte maturation, and the architecture of post-transcriptional regulatory networks operating through RNP complexes. The unique tractability of C. elegans — transparent germline, powerful genetic manipulation — enabled the detailed molecular dissection presented here.

## Significance in Introduction Context

- Provides a mechanistic account of translational regulation during a universal cell biological transition (meiotic maturation), exemplifying the reverse genetics toolkit (RNAi, loss-of-function mutants, genetic interaction analysis)
- Demonstrates that RNP complexes are the central logic gates controlling post-transcriptional gene expression at developmental transitions
- Why read this paper in class: it integrates modern genomics methods (RNA-seq, PAR-CLIP, PAT-seq) with classical C. elegans developmental genetics, serving as a methodological benchmark for systems-level analysis of translational control
