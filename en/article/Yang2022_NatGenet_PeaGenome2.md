# Improved Pea Reference Genome and Pan-Genome Highlight Genomic Features and Evolutionary Characteristics

## Citation (NLM)
Yang T, Liu R, Luo Y, et al. Improved pea reference genome and pan-genome highlight genomic features and evolutionary characteristics. Nat Genet. 2022;54(10):1553–1563. doi:10.1038/s41588-022-01172-2

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-022-01172-2](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-022-01172-2)

---

## Summary

This study reports a substantially improved pea reference genome (PeaZW6) for cultivar ZW6, a pan-genome of 116 cultivated and wild pea accessions, and a comprehensive population genomics analysis linking genomic diversity to domestication and to the molecular identity of Mendel's genetic loci.

**Improved reference genome PeaZW6**: Assembled de novo from PacBio SMRT reads (379 Gb, 85.2× coverage) combined with 10x Genomics scaffolding, BioNano optical mapping, Hi-C chromatin conformation capture, and Illumina sequencing. Contig N50 reached 8.98 Mb — a 243-fold improvement over PeaCaméor (0.037 Mb). The assembly spans 3,796.7 Mb; 97.96% of contigs were anchored into seven pseudomolecules (vs. 82.51% in PeaCaméor). Unknown sequence (Ns) shrank from 760.8 Mb to 10.3 Mb, demonstrating dramatically improved assembly of complex repeat regions. BUSCO genome-mode completeness improved from 96.78% to 99.38%. LTR retrotransposon assembly index (LAI) improved from 2.09 to 13.31, reflecting far more complete representation of full-length LTR elements that benefited from long-read assembly.

**Genome annotation**: 47,526 protein-coding genes were annotated. Genes with gaps in 3 kb flanking regions decreased from 20% to 1%, and transcripts per gene increased from 1.29 to 1.42, indicating improved annotation of regulatory regions and alternative splicing.

**Population genomics and structural variation**: Among 118 Pisum accessions, 26,250,039 SNPs, 1,443,829 small indels, and 376,309 structural variants (SVs ≥30 bp) were identified. Deletions comprised 94.5% of SVs, predominantly derived from LTR/Copia and LTR/Gypsy repeats. ADMIXTURE, phylogenetic trees (SNP and SV-based), and PCA analyses unanimously supported three distinct species in Pisum: P. fulvum, P. sativum (four genetic subgroups, PSI–IV), and P. abyssinicum — strongly supporting P. abyssinicum as an independent species, not a subspecies of P. sativum. The two cultivated P. sativum groups (PSII and PSIII) correspond to Asian and European cultivated peas, respectively, suggesting divergent transmission routes after domestication.

**Rediscovery of Mendel's genetic loci**: QTL analysis of 12 agronomic traits in a 300-member F2 population (WJ × ZW6) identified 25 QTLs. Three QTLs corresponding to Mendel's seed shape (SS3), stem length (SL5), and pod form (PF5) yielded LOD scores of 78.1, 53.1, and 31.9, with phenotypic variance explained of 68.7%, 46.7%, and 37.6%. Homology mapping confirmed the R gene (starch branching enzyme, SS3) and Le gene (gibberellin 3β-hydroxylase, SL5) as Mendel's seed-shape and stem-length genes, plus a candidate gene (Psat05G0794700) for pod form. Notably, in ZW6, the r allele carries a 9-bp exon insertion rather than the expected 0.8-kb insertion, producing pitted rather than wrinkled seeds.

**Pan-genome**: De novo assemblies from 116 Pisum accessions were clustered into 112,776 pan-genes. Core genes (15,470; 35.19%) were enriched in fundamental cellular functions, while variable genes were enriched in stress and stimulus responses. P. fulvum and P. abyssinicum harbor unique gene sets with functional enrichment for development/reproduction and stress response, respectively, highlighting their value as future breeding resources.

## Significance in Introduction Context

This paper's significance at the introduction of the field:
- [Elevates pea from an "orphan crop" to a fully genome-enabled model organism, building on Kreplak 2019 with dramatically improved assembly continuity and annotation quality]
- [Molecularly rediscovers Mendel's seed-shape and stem-length genes through QTL analysis in a reference cultivar, directly connecting 19th-century classical genetics to 21st-century genomics]
- [Essential reading for a genetics course because it introduces pan-genome concepts and structural variation in a crop-biology context, while the P. abyssinicum species status debate illustrates how genomic data resolves classical taxonomic controversies]

## Future Research Directions

Unresolved questions and future research ideas raised by this paper:

- [Three of Mendel's seven traits remain molecularly uncharacterized (pod color GP/gp, flower position Fa/fa, and pod form V/v beyond the candidate identified here); can PeaZW6 enable their positional cloning?]
- [What additional evidence will definitively resolve the domestication history of P. abyssinicum — was it independently domesticated from a distinct Ethiopian P. s. elatius ancestor, and when?]
- [The pan-genome was built from NGS-only individual assemblies, which the authors acknowledge as limited; how many additional structural variants and functional gene PAVs will emerge from long-read individual genome assemblies?]
