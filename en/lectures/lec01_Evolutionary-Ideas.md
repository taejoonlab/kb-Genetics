# Evolutionary Ideas

**Course:** BME333 / BIO333 Genetics (UNIST, 2026 Fall) · Lecture 01 · ~60 min
**Syllabus:** [← Course schedule](../../lectures/2026.BME333-BIO333-Syllabus.md) — Week 01 Mon, 2026-08-31

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:
- Explain how pre-Darwinian ideas of heredity (blending, pangenesis, use/disuse) framed the questions genetics later answered.
- State the core logic of Darwin's theory: descent with modification by natural selection, and its dependence on heritable variation.
- Describe why Darwin lacked a working theory of inheritance and how this "missing mechanism" limited his argument.
- Define adaptation and fitness in a modern, testable sense, and distinguish natural selection from inclusive fitness.
- Situate the Modern Synthesis as the reconciliation of Darwinian selection with Mendelian and population genetics.

## Lecture Outline (~60 min)
1. **Course introduction & the genetics–evolution link** (~8 min) — What this course covers, how it is organized (population → forward → reverse), and why evolution is the conceptual backbone of genetics: "nothing in biology makes sense except in the light of evolution."
2. **Heredity before genetics** (~12 min) — Pre-1900 ideas: blending inheritance, Lamarckian use/disuse and inheritance of acquired characters, and Darwin's own provisional theory of pangenesis (gemmules). Why blending inheritance is fatal to selection (variation is diluted away).
3. **Darwin's argument** (~12 min) — The syllogism of natural selection: heritable variation + differential reproduction → descent with modification. Artificial selection and horticulture as Darwin's evidence base; the gap where a mechanism of inheritance should be.
4. **What is adaptation? Fitness and selection** (~12 min) — Modern operational definitions of adaptation and fitness; adaptation as a product of selection versus incidental traits. Introduce inclusive fitness (Hamilton) as an extension explaining apparently altruistic behavior.
5. **Toward the Modern Synthesis** (~10 min) — How Mendelian genetics (rediscovered 1900), population genetics, and microbial/experimental evolution merged Darwinism with a real theory of inheritance.
6. **Social & historical context; wrap-up** (~6 min) — Reception and social implications of Darwin's ideas; preview of the tree of life (Lecture 02).
