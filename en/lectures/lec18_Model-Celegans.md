# Genetic Model — C. elegans

**Course:** BME333 / BIO333 Genetics (UNIST, 2026 Fall) · Lecture 18 · ~60 min
**Syllabus:** [← Course schedule](../../lectures/2026.BME333-BIO333-Syllabus.md) — Week 11 Wed, 2026-11-11

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:
- Explain why Sydney Brenner chose *C. elegans* and what features make it a premier metazoan genetic model (transparency, invariant cell lineage, hermaphroditic self-fertilization, short life cycle).
- Describe the complete cell lineage and the connectome as foundational resources.
- Outline how forward genetic screens in *C. elegans* identify genes controlling development, cell death, and behavior.
- Explain how the worm is used to dissect oocyte maturation, chromosome dynamics (e.g., topoisomerase II), and cell-cycle/tumor-suppressor pathways (e.g., LIN-35/Rb).
- Appreciate the worm's role as a disease-modeling and functional-genomics platform.

## Lecture Outline (~60 min)
1. **Brenner's vision and why the worm** (~10 min) — The rationale for a simple metazoan: defined anatomy, ~1000 somatic cells, self-fertilizing hermaphrodites for easy homozygosis, and rapid generation time.
2. **Foundational resources: lineage & connectome** (~10 min) — The invariant, fully mapped cell lineage and wiring diagram; how a fixed lineage enables single-cell genetic dissection.
3. **Forward genetics in the worm** (~12 min) — EMS mutagenesis, self-fertilization to recover recessives, balancer strategies, and landmark screens (cell death, vulval development, chemosensation).
4. **Chromosome dynamics and oocyte maturation** (~12 min) — Case studies: topoisomerase II (*top-2*) in chromosome segregation, and MSP/signaling control of oocyte maturation.
5. **Cell-cycle and tumor-suppressor pathways** (~10 min) — LIN-35 as the worm Rb ortholog; synthetic-lethal/genetic-interaction logic (synMuv) as a paradigm for pathway dissection.
6. **The worm as a disease and functional-genomics model** (~4 min) — RNAi feeding libraries, conserved pathways, and translation to human biology.
7. **Wrap-up & discussion** (~2 min).
