# Here, There, and Everywhere: Kinetochore Function on Holocentric Chromosomes

## Citation (NLM)
Dernburg AF. Here, There, and Everywhere: Kinetochore Function on Holocentric Chromosomes. J Cell Biol. 2001;153(6):F33–F38.

**DOI:** No formal DOI assigned. Journal of Cell Biology 2001 Commentary.

---

## Summary

This Commentary by Abby Dernburg (Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory), published in a focused issue of the *Journal of Cell Biology* in 2001, synthesizes three simultaneously published research articles (Moore and Roth; Oegema et al.; Howe et al.) alongside prior literature to provide a comprehensive overview of kinetochore function on holocentric chromosomes. Unlike monocentric chromosomes — which attach microtubules at a discrete centromere and migrate centromere-first during anaphase — holocentric chromosomes distribute kinetic activity along their entire length, moving broadside toward the pole. Dernburg situates the nematode *Caenorhabditis elegans* as the premier molecular genetic model for studying holocentric chromosome biology and highlights how the then-recent development of RNA interference (RNAi) transformed the field by enabling targeted depletion of essential kinetochore components.

The central finding emerging from the three companion papers is the striking evolutionary conservation of kinetochore protein composition between holocentric and monocentric organisms. *C. elegans* proteins — HCP-3 (CENP-A homolog), HCP-4 (CENP-C homolog), HCP-1/2 (CENP-F homologs), HIM-10 (Nuf2 homolog), BUB-1, and MCAK — all share similarity with centromere/kinetochore proteins from monocentric species, and their assembly follows the same hierarchical order (CENP-A recruits CENP-C, which recruits downstream components). A notable structural observation is that the "ribbon" of kinetochore staining distributed along prophase chromosomes condenses into a dot-like structure by metaphase — suggesting that holocentric kinetochores may not face fundamentally different geometrical constraints in achieving bipolar orientation on the spindle.

Dernburg also catalogues open questions unique to holocentric biology: how a distributed kinetochore avoids bipolar (merotelic) attachment; how achiasmate chromosomes accomplish reductional meiotic segregation without a defined centromere; and how karyotype stability is maintained despite somatic tolerability of chromosome fragmentation. While mitotically stable chromosome fragments can segregate normally in *C. elegans*, heterozygous rearrangements suppress meiotic recombination and reduce fertility — providing a population-level filter that eliminates most gross rearrangements from wild populations.

---

## Significance in Introduction Context
- Demonstrates that centromere identity is not universally determined by a discrete DNA sequence or locus but can be distributed epigenetically, expanding the conceptual framework for centromere function beyond the monocentric paradigm.
- The deep conservation of kinetochore protein composition between *C. elegans* holocentric chromosomes and monocentric eukaryotes provides key evidence that the core kinetochore machinery is an ancient, conserved molecular machine.
- An important entry point for understanding why the loss of individual kinetochore components produces different severity in mitosis versus meiosis, and why spindle geometry context matters for chromosome segregation fidelity.

## Future Research Directions
- Map HCP-3 (CENP-A) binding sites genome-wide by chromatin immunoprecipitation to determine whether holocentric centromere positioning has any sequence preference
- Elucidate the directionality mechanism by which a single chiasma triggers progressive loss of sister cohesion toward one chromosome end in *C. elegans* meiosis
- Compare kinetochore protein requirements between oocyte and spermatocyte meiosis to understand differential tolerance of kinetochore depletion
- Use comparative genomics across nematode species to understand how karyotype evolution proceeds in a holocentric lineage
- Investigate the structural features that prevent merotelic microtubule attachment by the condensed metaphase holocentric kinetochore
