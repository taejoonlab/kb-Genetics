# Minor Loops in Major Folds: Enhancer-Promoter Looping, Chromatin Restructuring, and Their Association with Transcriptional Regulation and Disease

## Citation (NLM)
Matharu N, Ahituv N. Minor loops in major folds: enhancer-promoter looping, chromatin restructuring, and their association with transcriptional regulation and disease. PLoS Genet. 2015;11(12):e1005640. doi:10.1371/journal.pgen.1005640

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1005640](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1005640)

---

## Summary

This review comprehensively examines how the three-dimensional folding of chromatin within the nucleus determines gene expression outcomes, with particular focus on the hierarchy from large chromosomal territories down to the fine-scale looping between individual enhancers and their target promoters. The authors frame this hierarchy with the conceptual metaphor embedded in the title: individual enhancer-promoter loops are "minor loops" that operate within the larger structural context of topologically associating domains (TADs), which in turn are the "major folds" of chromatin organization.

The review surveys the methods used to detect chromatin interactions. DNA-FISH can visualize specific long-range interactions (>100 kb) in individual cells, capturing cell-to-cell variability but limited to a few loci at a time. Chromosome Conformation Capture (3C) and its derivatives (4C, 5C) use formaldehyde crosslinking followed by restriction digestion and proximity ligation to detect chromatin contacts. Hi-C extends this genome-wide by incorporating biotinylated ligation junctions and paired-end sequencing. ChIA-PET adds a chromatin immunoprecipitation step to enrich for interactions mediated by specific proteins such as RNA Polymerase II, enabling identification of promoter-associated loops at higher resolution.

TADs are defined as genomic regions (typically hundreds of kilobases in size) with high internal interaction frequencies and sharp boundaries where interaction frequency drops. TAD boundaries are enriched for CTCF, cohesin-associated proteins, RNA Polymerase II, and H3K27me3. They are conserved between human and mouse and appear to partition the genome into regulatory units, constraining which enhancers can communicate with which promoters. TADs also overlap with DNA replication timing domains (constant timing regions), and their boundaries coincide with replication timing transition regions.

The review details the molecular determinants of enhancer-promoter specificity: looping factors (CTCF, cohesin, Ldb1 self-associating domain), gene regulatory elements that provide biochemical compatibility between interacting loci, and non-coding RNAs including enhancer RNAs (eRNAs) and lncRNAs. The paper then introduces the concept of "TADopathies" — human diseases caused by disruption of TAD boundaries. Examples include chromosomal inversions that juxtapose ectopic enhancers with oncogenes in leukemia, SNPs that attenuate enhancer-promoter looping at the OCA2 pigmentation locus, and 660-kb deletions that eliminate a TAD and cause enhancer adoption by the LMNB1 gene, leading to autosomal dominant adult-onset leukodystrophy. Structural variants at the chromosome 2q35-36 locus affect the boundaries between TADs containing WNT6/IHH, EPHA4, and PAX3, causing distinct limb malformation phenotypes depending on which boundary is disrupted.

## Significance in Introduction Context

- Establishes the hierarchical framework linking chromosome territories, TADs, and individual enhancer-promoter loops as nested levels of 3D genome organization
- Introduces "TADopathies" as a mechanistic concept for human genetic disease caused by disruption of chromatin domain boundaries rather than protein-coding sequences
- Explains how the same genomic sequence can drive tissue-specific gene expression through cell-type-specific chromatin looping, providing a molecular basis for spatiotemporal gene regulation
- Demonstrates that structural variants (inversions, deletions, duplications) can cause disease by rewiring enhancer-promoter interactions across TAD boundaries, with implications for interpreting non-coding human genetic variants
- Reviews how non-coding RNAs (eRNAs, lncRNAs) function not just as regulatory outputs but as active participants in stabilizing chromatin loops and directing enhancer specificity

## Future Research Directions

- Developing live-cell imaging approaches to capture the dynamics of enhancer-promoter looping in real time and understand how loop formation relates to transcriptional bursting
- Applying single-cell Hi-C and multi-omics technologies to understand the cell-to-cell variability in TAD structure and how this heterogeneity contributes to developmental plasticity
- Systematically cataloguing TADopathies by integrating structural variant databases with TAD boundary maps across cell types to predict disease-causing rearrangements
- Identifying additional looping factors and eRNA mechanisms that confer enhancer-promoter specificity beyond CTCF and cohesin
- Using dCas9 fusion proteins to artificially tether genomic loci and test the causal relationship between specific chromatin loops and gene expression
