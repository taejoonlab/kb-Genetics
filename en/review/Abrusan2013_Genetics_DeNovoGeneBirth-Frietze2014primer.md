# Educational Primer: De Novo Gene Birth (Frietze & Leatherman 2014)

## Citation (NLM)
Frietze S, Leatherman JL. An educational primer for genetics: de novo gene birth. Genetics. 2014;196(3):593-599. doi:10.1534/genetics.113.160895

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.113.160895](https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.113.160895)

**Original paper:** [[en/article/Abrusan2013_Genetics_DeNovoGeneBirth]]

---

## Background

This paper is an educational primer published in the *Genetics* journal Primer section, designed to accompany Abrusan (2013) on de novo gene birth. It explains the conceptual background, experimental approaches, and data interpretation strategies needed for students to engage with the original research article. The primer addresses the central concept that new genes can arise entirely from noncoding DNA through a proto-gene intermediate stage, which is distinct from gene duplication-based mechanisms of gene origination.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Explanation of phylogenetic profiling used to assign evolutionary ages to yeast genes
2. Introduction to bioinformatics tools for β-strand content prediction (PSIPRED) and aggregation propensity (TANGO, AGGRESCAN)
3. Overview of protein-protein interaction databases and their use in evolutionary analyses
4. Discussion questions and exercises for students to critically analyze the Abrusan (2013) data

---

## Results

- This primer does not generate independent experimental results; it reframes the key findings of Abrusan (2013) for educational use
- **Core educational content**: The stepwise model of de novo gene birth — noncoding DNA → transcription → translation → proto-gene → functional gene — is laid out clearly
- **Key emphasis**: Students are guided to understand why β-strand content and aggregation propensity decrease as genes age, as a product of evolutionary selection for stable, non-toxic proteins
- **Critical thinking**: Discussion questions help students analyze data figures from Abrusan (2013) and design follow-up experiments

---

## Perspective

Educational primers serve an important role in making cutting-edge research accessible at the undergraduate and graduate level. This primer is notable for going beyond summary to engage students with the conceptual novelty of de novo gene birth as a field that is actively rewriting evolutionary genetics textbooks. Connecting primary research to teaching is a valuable contribution that enables students to participate in live scientific debates.
