# Educational Primer for Field Cricket Speciation Study (Wittkopp 2013)

## Citation (NLM)

## Background

Patricia Wittkopp's educational primer accompanies Andres et al. (2013) and is designed to help students engage with population genetics and speciation research methods. Published in the *Genetics* education section, it explains key methodological concepts — SNP analysis, transcriptome comparison, and speciation metrics — and provides the biological context needed to interpret the cricket study results.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Introduction to RNA-seq for comparative transcriptomics
2. Explanation of fixed differences versus polymorphisms in SNP analysis; connection to speciation
3. Overview of population genetics statistics (Fst, dN/dS) used in speciation studies
4. Description of seminal fluid protein candidate identification via sequence homology
5. Biological background on *Gryllus* field cricket species and their hybrid zone

---

## Results

- This primer does not generate independent experimental results; it reframes and explains Andres et al. (2013) findings for educational use
- **Methodological core**: RNA-seq combined with SNP-based divergence measurement is established as the standard toolkit for modern speciation transcriptomics
- **Hypothesis testing logic**: How to statistically compare divergence rates between SFP and non-SFP gene classes is explained clearly
- **Cricket system**: Why the incomplete reproductive isolation between the two *Gryllus* species makes this an ideal "speciation in progress" system
- **Student activities**: Discussion questions and exercises for data interpretation and alternative hypothesis generation

---

## Perspective

This primer illustrates how modern comparative transcriptomics methods enable new approaches to classical evolutionary questions. The application of RNA-seq to non-model organisms has made genome-wide transcriptome divergence analysis feasible. Students gain exposure to population genetics methods applicable beyond traditional model organisms.
