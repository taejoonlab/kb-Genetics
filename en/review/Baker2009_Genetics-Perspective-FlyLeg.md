# Drosophila Leg Proximodistal Patterning: From Polar Coordinate Model to Boundary Model (Baker 2011)

## Citation (NLM)
Baker NE. Perspectives: from pole to pole: developmental genetics of Drosophila appendage proximodistal patterning. Genetics. 2011;187(4):1003-1010. doi:10.1534/genetics.111.127191

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.111.127191](https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.111.127191)

---

## Background

This Perspectives article by N.E. Baker reviews the history of research on how the proximodistal (PD) axis of the *Drosophila* leg is patterned during development. The 1970s polar coordinate model (French, Bryant, Bryant) proposed that cells carry positional information encoded as circular coordinates, and that intercalary growth occurs when non-adjacent cells meet. While this model elegantly explained regeneration and duplication experiments, it lacked molecular underpinnings. The subsequent identification of Wingless (Wg), Hedgehog (Hh), and Decapentaplegic (Dpp) signaling pathways led to the boundary model, which explains PD axis specification through signal gradients emanating from compartment boundaries.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Classical leg imaginal disc transplantation and recombination experiments that established the polar coordinate model
2. Expression pattern analysis of Wg, Hh, Dpp pathway components by in situ hybridization and immunostaining
3. Clonal analysis using the FLP-FRT system to assess phenotypes of signal pathway mutations
4. Misexpression experiments: ectopic expression of signaling molecules to induce ectopic leg structures
5. Mapping of transcription factor (Dll, dac, hth) expression domains to PD positions

---

## Results

- **Polar coordinate model achievements and limits**: Mathematically elegant description of regeneration/duplication data, but no molecular identity for positional information
- **Wg/Dpp boundary establishment**: Wg (ventral) and Dpp (dorsal) are expressed on opposite sides of the leg disc; the intersection of the two signals specifies the distal tip
- **Hh as upstream regulator**: Hh is expressed at the anterior/posterior boundary and induces both Dpp (dorsal) and Wg (ventral) signals
- **PD organizer transcription factors**: Dll, dac, and hth respond to Wg/Dpp gradient strength and are expressed in different PD domains, establishing the PD pattern
- **Boundary model**: Cells determine their PD position based on distance (signal gradient strength) from compartment boundaries

---

## Perspective

Baker's perspective illustrates how classical developmental genetics research was converted to mechanistic understanding through molecular biology. The polar coordinate model served as a phenomenological framework that was later replaced mechanistically by the Wg/Hh/Dpp boundary model. The roles of these signals are conserved across Drosophila appendages (wing, eye) and share evolutionary parallels with vertebrate limb development, exemplifying the value of model organism research.
