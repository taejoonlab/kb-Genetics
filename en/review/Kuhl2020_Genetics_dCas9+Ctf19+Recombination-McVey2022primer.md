# Beyond Editing: CRISPR/Cas9 for Protein Localization Studies — An Educational Primer

## Citation (NLM)
McVey SL, Olson MA, Pawlowski WP, Nannas NJ. Beyond editing, CRISPR/Cas9 for protein localization studies. Genetics. 2022 Sep;222(1):iyac109. doi:10.1093/genetics/iyac109.

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1093/genetics/iyac109](https://doi.org/10.1093/genetics/iyac109)

---

## Summary

This educational primer provides the background needed to read Kuhl et al. (2020), covering meiotic recombination biology, dCas9 technology, and key experimental methods at an introductory level.

**Chromosome segregation and meiosis**: The primer explains the distinction between mitosis and meiosis, with emphasis on homolog synapsis and crossover (CO) formation during meiosis I. COs serve two functions: (1) forming **chiasmata** that physically link homologs to ensure proper biorientation at the metaphase I plate; and (2) generating new allele combinations that increase genetic diversity. Recombination frequency is measured in **centimorgans (cM)**—1 cM equals 1 recombination event per 100 meioses.

**Pericentromeric recombination suppression**: COs near the centromere can disrupt kinetochore tension sensing, leading to chromosome nondisjunction. Cells have therefore evolved mechanisms to strongly suppress COs in pericentromeric regions. Understanding which kinetochore components mediate this suppression is the central question of Kuhl et al. (2020).

**CRISPR/dCas9 technology**: Catalytically dead Cas9 (dCas9) retains guide RNA-directed DNA binding but cannot cut DNA. When fused to a protein domain of interest, dCas9 serves as a programmable "molecular GPS" that can recruit any protein activity to a chosen genomic location. Kuhl et al. exploited this to recruit kinetochore complex subunits to ectopic non-centromeric loci and test their CO-suppressive capacity independently of the centromere.

**Tetrad analysis**: Yeast allows recovery and analysis of all four meiotic products from a single meiosis, enabling precise measurement of recombination frequencies and strand exchange patterns.

**Western blots, ChIP, and co-IP**: The primer explains protein expression verification (western blot), protein-DNA interaction analysis (chromatin immunoprecipitation, ChIP), and protein-protein interaction analysis (co-immunoprecipitation, co-IP), describing how each method is applied in the Kuhl et al. experiments.

## Significance in Introduction Context

- Introduces the use of dCas9 beyond genome editing—as a programmable protein recruitment platform—representing a conceptually important expansion of CRISPR technology
- Explains the experimental methods (cM measurement, tetrad analysis, ChIP) necessary to interpret the primary paper
- Provides systematic coverage of meiosis biology (crossovers, chiasmata, nondisjunction) at the level needed for the course
- Essential for this course because it provides all the background needed to engage critically with Kuhl et al. (2020) without prior specialist knowledge of kinetochore biology

- Compare pericentromeric CO suppression mechanisms across plant and animal species
