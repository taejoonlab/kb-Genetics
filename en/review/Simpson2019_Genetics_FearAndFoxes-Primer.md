# Simpson 2020 — Fear and Foxes: An Educational Primer on Behavioral Genetics

## Citation (NLM)
Simpson JH. Fear and foxes: an educational primer for use with "anterior pituitary transcriptome suggests differences in ACTH release in tame and aggressive animals". Genetics. 2020;215(1):15-24. doi:10.1534/genetics.120.303046

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.120.303046](https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.120.303046)

## Summary

This Educational Primer by Julie H. Simpson accompanies Hekman et al. (2018), which compared anterior pituitary transcriptomes between tame and aggressive Siberian foxes.

The primer introduces the Belyaev/Trut Siberian fox experiment begun in the 1950s in the Soviet Union. Over 50+ generations of selective breeding, distinct lines were established: one highly tame (dog-like) and one highly aggressive. The HPA axis (hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis) and the role of ACTH secreted from the anterior pituitary are explained in accessible terms.

RNA-seq analysis of the fox study found 18,940 genes expressed, with 346 differentially expressed genes (DEGs): 191 upregulated in aggressive foxes and 155 upregulated in tame foxes. Validation was performed by qRT-PCR, and gene ontology (GO) analysis highlighted exocytosis and Jak-STAT signaling pathways. Alternative splicing analysis was also conducted.

The primer also addresses ongoing debates in domestication biology including domestication syndrome, the neural crest hypothesis, and the neoteny hypothesis, and provides educational guidance on interpreting RNA-seq analyses.

## Significance in Introduction Context

- A classic example of how long-term selection experiments link behavioral phenotypes to gene expression
- Serves as an accessible introduction to comparative transcriptomics via RNA-seq in an evolutionary genetics context
- Provides a molecular evolutionary framework for understanding the genetic basis of domestication
