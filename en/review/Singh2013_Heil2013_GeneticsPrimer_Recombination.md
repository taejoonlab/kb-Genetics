# Heil & Noor 2013 — Genetics Primer: Meiotic Recombination

## Summary

This Genetics Primer by Caiti Smukowski Heil and Mohamed A.F. Noor accompanies Singh et al. (2013), which studied fine-scale recombination rate variation in the *Drosophila* garnet-scalloped region.

The primer explains the principles of a classical genetics cross using garnet and scalloped X-chromosome mutations. The Singh et al. study screened 92,105 male progeny and identified 6,716 recombinants (overall rate of 7.3 cM), demonstrating the precision achievable with classical mapping approaches.

For fine-scale resolution, the companion study used target-enriched shotgun sequencing to genotype 451 markers across a 2.1 Mb region, combined with pooled DNA sequencing (100 flies per pool, 25 pools). The result was a 90-fold range of recombination rates at the 5 kb scale (0.3–27 cM/Mb). Correlations between recombination rate variation and local sequence features are discussed.

The primer demonstrates how integrating classical genetics cross design with next-generation sequencing can reveal the fine-scale architecture of recombination, which is otherwise invisible to lower-resolution approaches.

---

## Significance in Introduction Context

- Demonstrates empirically that recombination is far from uniform and shows dramatic local variation across the genome
- Illustrates the methodological integration of classical genetic crosses with NGS, an important pedagogical model
- Provides foundational understanding for recombination hotspots and their implications for genome evolution
