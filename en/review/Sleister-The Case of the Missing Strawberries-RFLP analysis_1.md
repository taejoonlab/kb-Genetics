# Sleister 2014 — The Case of the Missing Strawberries: RFLP Analysis Lesson

## Citation (NLM)
Sleister HM. The case of the missing strawberries: an inquiry-based lesson using RFLP analysis. CourseSource. 2014;1. doi:10.24918/cs.2014.10

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.24918/cs.2014.10](https://doi.org/10.24918/cs.2014.10)

## Summary

This educational resource by Heidi Major Sleister presents a 4-hour guided-inquiry lesson that uses a fictional mystery — the disappearance of garden strawberries — to teach RFLP (Restriction Fragment Length Polymorphism) analysis.

The lesson sequence includes: DNA isolation from strawberries, restriction enzyme digestion (EcoRI and PstI on plasmid DNAs from the BioRad kit), a paper simulation of RFLP analysis for conceptual scaffolding, and hands-on agarose gel electrophoresis. The BioRad Forensic DNA Fingerprinting Kit (#166-0007EDU) is used with plasmids S1–S5 (S3 as control).

Key concepts taught include: the cell-chromosome-DNA relationship, the mechanism and function of restriction enzymes, the principles of gel electrophoresis, the rationale for PCR amplification prior to RFLP analysis, and the concept of polymorphisms. The narrative mystery format is designed to increase student engagement and motivation.

The lesson makes forensic genetics principles accessible in the classroom, employing active learning methodology that combines hands-on experiments with group discussion.

## Significance in Introduction Context

- An effective inquiry-based educational model for teaching the principles of DNA polymorphism analysis
- RFLP is a foundational molecular genetics technique and a historical predecessor to modern SNP analysis
- Combines inquiry-based learning with narrative engagement to increase motivation in genetics education
