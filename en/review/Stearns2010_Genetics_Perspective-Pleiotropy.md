# Stearns 2010 — One Hundred Years of Pleiotropy: A Retrospective

## Citation (NLM)

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.110.122549](https://doi.org/10.1534/genetics.110.122549)

## Summary

This Perspectives article by Frank W. Stearns is a retrospective review written on the centennial of Ludwig Plate's coining of "Pleiotropie" in 1910.

The paper traces the conceptual history of pleiotropy: Gruneberg's distinction between genuine and spurious pleiotropy; the Beadle-Tatum one gene/one enzyme hypothesis; Hadorn's distinction between mosaic and relational pleiotropy; and Williams' theory of antagonistic pleiotropy and its connection to the evolution of senescence. These landmarks show how the concept evolved from a descriptive term to a mechanistic and evolutionary framework.

Molecular mechanisms underlying pleiotropy are reviewed: overlapping reading frames, alternative splicing, mRNA editing, and multifunctional proteins. Quantitative estimates from network analyses and QTL studies suggest that genes affect an average of 4–8 traits, supporting modular rather than universal pleiotropy.

Key unanswered questions identified include: the full extent of pleiotropy, whether multiple primary gene products are common, and whether pleiotropy should be seen primarily as an evolutionary constraint or opportunity.
