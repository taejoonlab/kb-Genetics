## Summary

This PRIMER by Deborah Thurtle-Schmidt and Te-Wen Lo accompanies Tsukamoto et al. (2017), which investigated the regulation of oocyte meiotic maturation in *C. elegans*.

The primer explains why the C. elegans germline is an ideal model for studying oocyte development: transparent body enabling direct cell observation, DAPI visualization of meiotic stages, and the fact that MSP (major sperm protein) functions both as a cytoskeletal element and as a hormone triggering oocyte meiotic maturation. The central conundrum introduced is that both LIN-41 and OMA proteins (OMA-1, OMA-2) repress CDK-1 activation via 3'-UTR-dependent translational repression of cdc-25.3 mRNA, yet their mutant phenotypes are opposite.

The primer focuses on educating students about bioinformatic methods: RNA-seq library construction, FPKM normalization, principal component analysis (PCA), volcano plots, violin plots, sequence logos, and PAT-seq for poly(A) tail length quantification. The role of GLD-2 poly(A) polymerase in translational regulation of LIN-41-associated transcripts is also discussed.

For classroom use, the Jigsaw cooperative learning method is proposed, dividing students into four expert groups covering gene manipulation, protein experiments, whole-genome sequencing methods, and bioinformatics.

## Significance in Introduction Context

- Clearly demonstrates the key role of post-transcriptional regulation in developmental processes using the C. elegans system
- Provides accessible educational explanation of how high-throughput sequencing and bioinformatics enhance classical genetic analysis
- An experimental model for understanding the connection between RNA-binding protein complexes and meiotic regulation
