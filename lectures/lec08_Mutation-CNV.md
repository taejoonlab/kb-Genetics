# Mutation & Copy Number Variation

**Course:** BME333 / BIO333 Genetics (UNIST, 2026 Fall) · Lecture 08 · ~60 min
**Syllabus:** [← Course schedule](2026.BME333-BIO333-Syllabus.md) — Week 04 Wed, 09-23

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:
- Define mutation and classify mutations by molecular type (point, indel, structural, copy-number) and by functional effect.
- Explain the Luria–Delbrück fluctuation test and what it proved about the origin (pre-existing vs. induced) of mutations.
- Describe major mutagenesis mechanisms and how mutation became an experimental, controllable variable.
- Explain the Ames test and the principle of using reversion to screen for mutagens/carcinogens.
- Describe copy-number variation and transposable elements as sources of genomic variation and disease.

## Lecture Outline (~60 min)
1. **What is a mutation?** (~10 min) — Spontaneous vs. induced; substitutions, indels, frameshifts, structural and copy-number changes; loss- vs. gain-of-function and the germline/somatic distinction.
2. **The Luria–Delbrück fluctuation test** (~14 min) — The landmark experiment showing bacterial resistance mutations arise randomly *before* selection, not in response to it; discuss the statistics of the fluctuation analysis and its conceptual weight.
3. **Mutation as an experimental tool** (~10 min) — How mutagenesis (radiation, chemicals) turned mutation into a research strategy; historical arc from Muller onward.
4. **Detecting mutagens: the Ames test** (~10 min) — Reversion of *Salmonella his⁻* auxotrophs with S9 activation as a rapid mutagen/carcinogen screen; the mutagenesis–carcinogenesis link.
5. **Copy-number variation** (~8 min) — CNVs as a major class of human genomic variation; mechanisms (NAHR, replication errors) and phenotypic/disease consequences; how genomic variation is analyzed.
6. **Transposable elements** (~8 min) — TEs as mobile mutagens driving insertions, structural variation, and disease; their genomic scale and regulation.
