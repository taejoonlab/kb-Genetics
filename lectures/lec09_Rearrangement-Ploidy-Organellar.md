# Rearrangement, Ploidy & Organellar Inheritance

**Course:** BME333 / BIO333 Genetics (UNIST, 2026 Fall) · Lecture 09 · ~60 min
**Syllabus:** [← Course schedule](2026.BME333-BIO333-Syllabus.md) — Week 05 Wed, 09-30

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:
- Classify the major chromosomal rearrangements (deletions, duplications, inversions, translocations) and predict their meiotic and phenotypic consequences.
- Distinguish euploidy from aneuploidy and explain how nondisjunction generates aneuploids such as trisomies.
- Compare autopolyploidy and allopolyploidy and evaluate the evolutionary advantages and costs of whole-genome duplication.
- Describe organellar (mitochondrial/chloroplast) inheritance and contrast it with Mendelian nuclear inheritance (maternal inheritance, heteroplasmy, cytoplasmic segregation).

## Lecture Outline (~60 min)
1. **Chromosomal rearrangements** (~15 min) — Deletions, duplications, inversions (paracentric vs pericentric), and reciprocal/Robertsonian translocations; how heterozygotes form loops or crosses at meiosis and generate unbalanced gametes, semisterility, and altered linkage.
2. **Aneuploidy** (~12 min) — Nondisjunction in meiosis I vs II; monosomy and trisomy; dosage sensitivity; human examples (Down, Turner, Klinefelter); aneuploidy as a source of non-genetic cell-to-cell variability.
3. **Ploidy and whole-genome duplication** (~15 min) — Euploid variants; autopolyploidy vs allopolyploidy; sterility of odd ploidy; polyploidy in plant evolution and crop domestication; developmental/somatic polyploidy (endoreplication, wound response).
4. **Advantages and disadvantages of being polyploid** (~8 min) — Buffering, heterosis, and gene redundancy versus meiotic/mitotic instability and epigenetic remodeling after duplication.
5. **Organellar inheritance** (~10 min) — Uniparental (usually maternal) transmission; heteroplasmy and the mtDNA bottleneck; cytoplasmic segregation; why organellar traits break Mendelian ratios.
