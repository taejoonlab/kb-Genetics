# Genetic Model — E. coli and Bacteria

**Course:** BME333 / BIO333 Genetics (UNIST, 2026 Fall) · Lecture 17 · ~60 min
**Syllabus:** [← Course schedule](2026.BME333-BIO333-Syllabus.md) — Week 11 Mon, 2026-11-09

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:
- Explain why *E. coli* and its phages became the workhorse of molecular genetics (rapid growth, haploid genome, easy selection).
- Distinguish the three modes of horizontal gene transfer in bacteria: conjugation, transduction, and transformation.
- Describe how conjugation and interrupted-mating mapping revealed the circular *E. coli* chromosome.
- Explain lysogeny and site-specific prophage integration (the Campbell model) and how phage genetics established the fine structure of the gene.
- Connect classic bacterial genetics to modern genome engineering and the "microbes in the Modern Synthesis" perspective.

## Lecture Outline (~60 min)
1. **Why bacteria? The rise of the microbial model** (~8 min) — Short generation time, defined media, selectable markers, and a single haploid chromosome make bacteria ideal for high-resolution genetics; frame how microbes were folded into the Modern Synthesis.
2. **Mutation is spontaneous: fluctuation and pre-existing variants** (~8 min) — Recap the Luria–Delbrück experiment as the founding demonstration that resistance mutations arise before selection, not in response to it.
3. **Conjugation and chromosome mapping** (~12 min) — Lederberg's discovery of recombination in *E. coli*; F factor, Hfr strains, and interrupted-mating to establish gene order and the circular chromosome.
4. **Transduction: phage-mediated gene transfer** (~10 min) — Zinder & Lederberg's discovery in *Salmonella*; generalized vs. specialized transduction and its use for fine mapping.
5. **Lysogeny and prophage integration** (~10 min) — The Campbell model of site-specific λ integration/excision; lysogenic vs. lytic decision.
6. **Phage genetics and the fine structure of the gene** (~8 min) — Amber (nonsense) mutants in phage T4, conditional lethals, and how phage crosses defined recombination within a gene; nod to modern *E. coli* genome engineering.
7. **Wrap-up & discussion** (~4 min) — From classical selection to designer genomes.

Reviews & articles to introduce in class (each has a bilingual en/ko pair):
- [Lederberg1987_Genetics_EcoliRecombination](../en/review/Lederberg1987_Genetics_EcoliRecombination.md) — first-hand account of discovering recombination/conjugation in *E. coli*; anchors the chromosome-mapping story.
- [LuriaDelbruck1943_Genetics_VirusResistance](../en/article/LuriaDelbruck1943_Genetics_VirusResistance.md) — the fluctuation test; mutations are spontaneous and pre-selective — the founding quantitative experiment.
- [Zinder1992_Genetics_BacterialTransduction](../en/review/Zinder1992_Genetics_BacterialTransduction.md) — discovery of transduction; introduces phage-mediated gene transfer for mapping.
- [Campbell1993_Genetics_ProphageInsertion](../en/review/Campbell1993_Genetics_ProphageInsertion.md) — the Campbell model of site-specific prophage integration; core of the lysogeny segment.
- [Parkinson1987_Genetics_BehavioralGenetics-Bacteria](../en/review/Parkinson1987_Genetics_BehavioralGenetics-Bacteria.md) — bacterial chemotaxis as "behavioral genetics," showing bacteria as a model for signaling and behavior.
- [Stahl1992_Genetics_UnicornsRevisited](../en/review/Stahl1992_Genetics_UnicornsRevisited.md) — reflections on phage recombination and the logic of genetic crosses.
- [Stahl1995_Genetics_AmberMutants-PhageT4](../en/review/Stahl1995_Genetics_AmberMutants-PhageT4.md) — amber (nonsense) conditional mutants in T4; how phage genetics dissected gene function.
- [Novick2019_PLoSGenet_MicrobesModernSynthesis](../en/review/Novick2019_PLoSGenet_MicrobesModernSynthesis.md) — historical/conceptual framing of how microbes entered evolutionary genetics.
- [Shapiro2009_Genetics_Perspective-Ecoli-GenomeEng](../en/review/Shapiro2009_Genetics_Perspective-Ecoli-GenomeEng.md) — from classical *E. coli* genetics toward genome engineering; bridges to the modern era.
