# Genetic Model — Fruitfly (Drosophila)

**Course:** BME333 / BIO333 Genetics (UNIST, 2026 Fall) · Lecture 19 · ~60 min
**Syllabus:** [← Course schedule](2026.BME333-BIO333-Syllabus.md) — Week 12 Mon, 2026-11-16

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:
- Explain why *Drosophila melanogaster* became the founding model for chromosomal and developmental genetics.
- Trace how the Morgan group used *Drosophila* (white gene, nondisjunction) to prove the chromosome theory of heredity and build the first genetic maps.
- Describe how meiotic-mutant and mapping tools established recombination and gene order.
- Explain how homeotic genes (Antennapedia/Bithorax complexes) revealed the genetic control of body plan.
- Appreciate the modern use of *Drosophila* for disease modeling (e.g., ALS modifier screens).

## Lecture Outline (~60 min)
1. **Morgan's fly room: birth of a model** (~10 min) — Why the fly (fast generations, few chromosomes, cheap culture); Sturtevant and the first linkage map; the culture of the fly room.
2. **The white gene and the chromosome theory** (~10 min) — The founding *white* mutation and sex-linked inheritance; a century of lessons from a single gene.
3. **Nondisjunction as proof of chromosome theory** (~10 min) — Bridges' nondisjunction work providing physical proof that genes reside on chromosomes.
4. **Recombination, linkage, and meiotic mutants** (~8 min) — Meiotic-mutant screens in *Drosophila* dissecting the machinery of recombination and chromosome segregation.
5. **Genetic control of development: homeotic genes** (~12 min) — Morgan's turn to development; the Antennapedia complex, Ed Lewis and the Bithorax complex, and the homeotic paradigm of body-plan genetics; the "homunculus"/fly-leg patterning perspectives.
6. **Modern Drosophila: disease modeling** (~7 min) — Genetic modifier screens (e.g., ALS) exploiting conserved pathways and powerful fly genetics.
7. **Wrap-up & discussion** (~3 min).
