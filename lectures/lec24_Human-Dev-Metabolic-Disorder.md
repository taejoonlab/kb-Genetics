# Human Genetics — Developmental & Metabolic Disorder

**Course:** BME333 / BIO333 Genetics (UNIST, 2026 Fall) · Lecture 24 · ~60 min
**Syllabus:** [← Course schedule](2026.BME333-BIO333-Syllabus.md) — Week 14, 2026-12-02 (Wed)

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:
- Explain how a single-gene defect in a metabolic enzyme produces disease, tracing the "one gene–one enzyme" logic from Garrod's inborn errors to modern inherited metabolic disorders.
- Classify inborn errors of metabolism (intoxication, energy, and storage/complex-molecule groups) and explain the rationale for newborn screening (e.g., PKU).
- Describe how developmental disorders arise from disruption of genes controlling embryonic development, including the major role of de novo mutations.
- Distinguish simple Mendelian inheritance from oligogenic/modifier effects and variable expressivity in human disease.
- Connect genotype to phenotype and to therapeutic/dietary intervention for treatable disorders.

## Lecture Outline (~60 min)
1. **From Garrod to genomes: the one gene–one enzyme foundation** (~10 min) — Garrod's "inborn errors of metabolism" (alkaptonuria) as the first link between a gene and a metabolic block; Beadle-Tatum and the one gene–one enzyme hypothesis; enzyme deficiency → substrate accumulation / product deficiency.
2. **Inborn errors of metabolism: logic and classification** (~10 min) — Individually rare but collectively common; pathophysiologic grouping into intoxication disorders, energy-metabolism defects, and storage/complex-molecule disorders; how the position of the enzyme block predicts the clinical picture.
3. **Phenylketonuria as a model IEM** (~8 min) — PAH deficiency, phenylalanine accumulation, and neurodevelopmental damage; why PKU is the archetype of a *treatable* genetic disease and the origin of newborn screening.
4. **Newborn screening & treatment principles** (~8 min) — Tandem mass spectrometry–based population screening; the four therapeutic strategies (substrate reduction, supplying deficient product, removing toxic metabolites, enhancing residual enzyme); dietary management.
5. **Genetics of developmental disorders** (~10 min) — Genes controlling embryonic patterning and organogenesis; congenital malformations and neurodevelopmental disorders; the outsized role of *de novo* mutations and the impact of trio exome/genome sequencing on diagnosis.
6. **Beyond simple Mendel: modifiers and expressivity** (~8 min) — Oligogenic inheritance and modifier loci (e.g., Bardet-Biedl syndrome); variable expressivity, incomplete penetrance, and why the same genotype yields different phenotypes.
7. **Synthesis & clinical outlook** (~6 min) — Genotype-to-phenotype reasoning across metabolic and developmental disease; diagnostic yield, counseling, and the move toward genome-based diagnosis.
